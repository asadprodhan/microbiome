# Overview

The **Bioinformatics Capability & Research Training Initiative (BCRTI)** is a structured Higher Degree by Research (HDR) training initiative designed to address one of the most significant bottlenecks in modern research training:

> The gap between generating large-scale sequencing data and independently transforming that data into publishable scientific outputs.

BCRTI was developed to accelerate HDR progression by providing practical, outcome-driven bioinformatics training built around authentic research workflows and real datasets.

The initiative enables researchers to move from:

```text
Raw sequencing files → Biological interpretation → Publication-ready outputs
```

within a structured micro-credential framework.

---

# Why BCRTI Exists

Modern HDR candidates increasingly generate large-scale sequencing datasets but often lack the computational capability required for independent analysis.

This commonly results in:

- Delayed HDR progression
- Reduced publication output
- Dependence on external bioinformatics support
- Reduced analytical confidence
- Long delays between data generation and manuscript preparation

# Learning Outcomes

By the end of this micro-credential, participants should be able to:

1. Explain key microbiome and sequencing concepts relevant to modern biological research.

2. Distinguish between amplicon sequencing and shotgun metagenomics workflows.

3. Interpret sequencing output files including FASTQ data and metadata structures.

4. Execute core microbiome bioinformatics workflows using QIIME2, DADA2, and R.

5. Conduct quality control, taxonomic analysis, diversity analysis, and statistical testing.

6. Generate publication-ready visualisations and analytical outputs.

7. Apply reproducible research practices to their own datasets.

8. Independently progress sequencing-based research projects toward publication.

# Week 1 — The Microbiome & How We Study It

## Format
Theoretical Lecture

## Week Overview

This session introduces the conceptual foundations of microbiome research and sequencing-based analysis.

Participants are introduced to:

- What microbiomes are
- Why microbiomes matter
- How sequencing enables microbiome analysis
- Amplicon sequencing vs shotgun metagenomics
- The wet-lab to bioinformatics pipeline
- FASTQ files and sequencing outputs

---

## Week 1 Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, participants should be able to:

- Define microbiome-related terminology
- Explain the role of 16S rRNA sequencing
- Distinguish between amplicon and shotgun workflows
- Understand how sequencing data are generated
- Interpret the structure and purpose of FASTQ files
- Match sequencing approaches to research questions

---

## Week 1 Skills & Knowledge

### Conceptual Knowledge
- Microbiome ecology
- Marker genes
- Sequencing strategies
- Experimental logic in microbiome research

### Practical Literacy
- FASTQ familiarity
- Workflow interpretation
- Understanding sequencing pipelines
