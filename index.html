Overview

Build confidence in microbiome data analysis

Microbiome sequencing is now widely used across agriculture, environmental science, biotechnology, health, veterinary science and molecular biology. Yet many researchers reach a difficult point after sequencing: transforming raw data into reliable, interpretable and publication-ready results.

This micro-credential provides a structured pathway through microbiome analysis, beginning with the biological and sequencing concepts behind microbiome studies and progressing into practical data analysis, visualisation and scientific reporting. Participants learn how to work with microbiome datasets in a reproducible way and how to communicate findings through figures, methods and results text.

What you will learn

Practical skills for microbiome research

Microbiome study design

  • Understand microbiome concepts and ecological foundations
  • Learn sequencing strategies used in microbiome studies
  • Understand metadata and experimental structure
  • Differentiate amplicon and shotgun metagenomics approaches

Bioinformatics workflows

  • Perform sequencing quality control
  • Understand denoising and ASV generation
  • Assign microbial taxonomy
  • Interpret taxonomic composition
  • Carry out downstream microbiome analysis

Statistical interpretation

  • Explore alpha diversity and beta diversity
  • Interpret ordination analyses (NMDS / PCoA)
  • Apply microbiome statistical testing
  • Perform differential abundance analysis
  • Interpret microbiome results in a research context
Course objectives

By the end of this micro-credential, participants will be able to:

01
Understand microbiome study workflows
  • Explain the major stages of a microbiome sequencing study
  • Move from biological question to analysis-ready data
  • Understand microbiome workflow logic and study design
02
Prepare reproducible microbiome projects
  • Prepare microbiome project files and metadata
  • Organise analysis inputs for reproducible workflows
  • Apply good project structure and documentation practices
03
Perform microbiome bioinformatics analysis
  • Perform sequencing quality control
  • Understand denoising and ASV generation
  • Carry out taxonomic classification and interpretation
04
Generate research-ready outputs
  • Generate diversity and ordination analyses
  • Interpret taxonomy and differential abundance outputs
  • Create publication-ready microbiome figures

How it works

A structured six-week learning pathway

1

Learn the concepts

Weeks 1–2 introduce microbiome biology, sequencing approaches, metadata, study design and analysis workflows.

2

Analyse the data

Weeks 3–5 guide participants through quality control, denoising, taxonomy, diversity analysis and differential abundance.

3

Prepare outputs

Week 6 focuses on visualisation, interpretation, figure assembly and writing microbiome Methods and Results sections.

Course outcomes

What participants will produce

  • A reproducible microbiome analysis project structure
  • Quality control summaries and documented analysis decisions
  • ASV tables, taxonomy outputs and taxonomic composition plots
  • Alpha and beta diversity analysis outputs
  • Ordination and statistical testing figures
  • Differential abundance analysis outputs
  • Publication-ready microbiome visualisations
  • Draft Methods and Results text for thesis or manuscript use
