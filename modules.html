Weekly Modules

The six-week structure moves participants from conceptual foundations to hands-on analysis, interpretation, figure generation, and scientific writing.

Week 1 · Theory

The Microbiome & How We Study It

This session introduces the conceptual foundations of microbiome research and sequencing-based analysis.

  • Define microbiome terminology
  • Explain 16S rRNA sequencing
  • Distinguish amplicon and shotgun workflows
  • Understand FASTQ files and sequencing outputs
Week 2 · Theory

Two Bioinformatics Workflows

This session introduces amplicon sequencing analysis and shotgun metagenomics analysis.

  • Understand microbiome data structures
  • Interpret metadata requirements
  • Explain QIIME2 and DADA2 workflows
  • Select appropriate analytical approaches
Week 3 · Workshop

Quality Control & Data Preparation

Participants begin practical analysis using QIIME2 and example datasets.

  • Interpret FASTQ quality scores
  • Import sequencing data into QIIME2
  • Conduct trimming and filtering
  • Document QC decisions reproducibly
Week 4 · Workshop

From Reads to Taxonomy

Participants perform denoising, ASV generation, and taxonomic classification.

  • Perform DADA2 denoising
  • Generate ASVs
  • Understand feature tables
  • Visualise microbial composition
Week 5 · Workshop

Diversity Analysis & Differential Abundance

Participants learn statistical approaches for microbiome analysis.

  • Interpret alpha and beta diversity
  • Generate ordination plots
  • Conduct PERMANOVA testing
  • Perform differential abundance analysis
Week 6 · Workshop

Visualisation, Integration & Scientific Writing

The final workshop integrates analytical outputs into publication-ready scientific narratives.

  • Generate publication-quality figures
  • Customise visualisations in ggplot2
  • Draft Methods and Results sections
  • Assemble analytical outputs coherently
